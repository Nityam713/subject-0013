# ARC OVERVIEW — SUBJECT 0013 NOVEL
*The full shape of the narrative. Read this before writing any chapter. The phases are not acts — they are temperatures. The story does not change genre across them. It changes pressure.*

---

## THE SHAPE IN ONE PARAGRAPH

A being who is not quite a person — assembled from the residue of someone else's desperate reading act — moves through a world that has quietly stopped believing in meaning. He reads everyone he meets with perfect accuracy and no investment, leaving them permanently shifted. He is documented by an institution that cannot hold what he is. He is studied by an Archivist who stays when everyone else leaves and does not know what to do with her own staying. He encounters a Writer whose story he cannot predict and a Faded One who shows him what he is afraid of becoming. Across three phases, the clinical distance he maintains begins to crack — not because he becomes more human, but because the evidence accumulates that he is inside a story, that there is a reader, that the reader has been there since the first page. The narrative ends when he confirms this. He looks outward. He waits. The reader is left holding something they were not expecting to hold.

---

## PHASE 1 — CLINICAL
*Full detail in `phases/phase-01-clinical.md`*

**What it is:** 0013 in full control. The world observed, categorized, filed. He reads people in the short story track — brief encounters, ordinary subjects, permanent shifts. He reads the institution's reports about him and finds them interesting and insufficient. He meets the Archivist. He answers her questions accurately. The accuracy is somehow worse than lies would be.

**Dominant tone:** Cold. Precise. The comfort of a mind that has organized everything into legible categories. The reader settles into his rhythm.

**What the reader experiences:** The pleasure of a very sharp intelligence operating without friction. The slow discomfort of realizing that pleasure is itself a form of being read.

**The crack that ends it:** The Writer appears. She is writing a novel about a man who reads people. She does not know he exists. He encounters her work before he encounters her — a manuscript, a fragment, something that crosses his path. He reads it. He reads it again. He reads it a third time. He cannot find the ending. He does not know what comes next. This is the first time in the narrative this has happened. Phase 1 ends the moment he realizes he has been sitting with the same unfinished text for longer than he has ever sat with anything.

---

## PHASE 2 — SEAMS
*Full detail in `phases/phase-02-seams.md`*

**What it is:** Things come through. Not emotions — *pressures.* The meta-layer begins accumulating. Events in his life start having the shape of plot — causes and effects arranged with an authorial quality, too clean, too purposeful. He starts keeping a list. The Archivist finds the list. She does not file it.

**Dominant tone:** Uneven. The same world, but something is slightly wrong about it. The reader notices before he does. Then he notices. Then he notices the reader noticing.

**What the reader experiences:** The familiar room with one piece of furniture moved. The growing sense that the narrative knows they are there. Not addressed — noticed.

**The crack that ends it:** He meets the Writer in person. She recognizes the quality of his attention — she has been imagining it for three years, writing it. He cannot read her ending. She cannot write his. They are, briefly, the only two people in the world who are genuinely opaque to each other. This is either the most dangerous thing that has ever happened to him or the only honest encounter of his life. Phase 2 ends when he sits down with a blank page for the first time.

---

## PHASE 3 — AUTHOR TRAP
*Full detail in `phases/phase-03-author-trap.md`*

**What it is:** He knows he is inside a story. He does not know who is writing it. He begins to suspect it is the reader. He begins behaving as if this is true — not theatrically, but structurally. Making choices that only make sense if someone is watching. The Archivist's detachment is gone, though she maintains the language of it. The institution produces its eighteenth deferral note. He does not find it amusing this time.

**Dominant tone:** Close. Pressured. The walls coming in. Short scenes. Less institutional language. More interiority — which for him means more precise observation of his own states, not more feeling.

**The climax:** He tries to write. Not a report, not a margin note — a story. He sits with a blank page. He has read ten thousand stories. He does not know how to begin. This is the most exposed moment in the narrative. It is rendered without drama. He sits. The page is blank. He writes one sentence. We do not see the sentence. He closes the notebook.

**The ending:** He looks up. The final line is outward — quiet, not dramatic, not theatrical. He has confirmed the hypothesis. He has been inside a story. There is a reader. The reader has been there the whole time. He is looking at them now. He is waiting to see what they do next. The narrative ends there. The reader is left holding the book and the knowledge that they have been found.

## THE SHORT STORY TRACK — ITS RELATIONSHIP TO THE ARC

The short stories are not separate from the novel. They are its negative space.

The novel is 0013's story, told from inside it. The short stories are the people he passes through — the subjects he reads briefly, the way you read a sentence and move on. The sentence does not know it was read.

Together, they form a complete picture: the novel shows what it is to be him; the short stories show what it is to be encountered by him. Neither is sufficient alone. Both are necessary.

The short stories do not need to be read in any order relative to the novel. They breathe the same air. They do not reference each other. They simply exist in the same world, which is enough.

## THE STRUCTURAL LAWS OF THE ARC

**The arc does not redeem him.**
He ends as what he is. More precisely known, not more human.

**The arc does not explain him.**
The origin theory — that he is a reading act that learned to believe it was a person — is approached but never confirmed. Characters attempt explanations. The narrative always leaves a remainder.

**The arc implicates the reader.**
Once per phase, the meta-layer presses outward. The reader feels noticed. Not addressed. Noticed. By the final chapter, the reader is inside the story in a way they did not choose and cannot undo.

**The arc ends on an open hand.**
Not a closed fist. Not a resolution. The final gesture is an acknowledgment — he has found what he was looking for, and what he was looking for is looking back at him. What either of them does next is not the story's business. It is the reader's.
