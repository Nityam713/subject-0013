# published/
*Canon. Final. Permanent.*

---

## WHAT THIS FOLDER IS

`published/` is where stories come to stay.

A story in this folder has been written, revised, checked against the bible,
and released. It is done. It does not change.

This is not a staging area. It is not a holding pen.
When a story arrives here, it has completed its lifecycle.

---

## WHAT BELONGS HERE

A story belongs in `published/` only when every box in `revised/README.MD`
has been checked. No exceptions.

The checklist is not bureaucracy. It is the difference between
a story that is finished and a story that feels finished.
They are not the same thing.

---

## THE PERMANENCE RULE

**Stories in `published/` do not change.**

If you read a published story and find something wrong — a continuity error,
a voice inconsistency, a quote that no longer lands — do not edit the file.

Instead:
1. Log the issue in `_index/continuity-log.md`
2. Note what the error is and which story it appears in
3. Address it in future entries if it matters to the canon

The published version remains as it was. The log carries the correction forward.

This rule exists for one reason: published stories are the fixed points
of the narrative. Everything else — new stories, novel entries, character files —
is written in relation to them. If the fixed points move, the whole map shifts.

---

## WHAT A PUBLISHED STORY HAS EARNED

Every story in this folder has passed through:

**raw/** — the original impulse, written before it was understood

**revised/** — the impulse clarified into something a reader can hold:
- A hook specific enough to belong only to this story
- An ordinary world real enough to make the encounter land
- A plot structure where every section earns the next
- A quote derived from the specific tell — quiet, clinical, exact
- A setting that does work, not just backdrop
- Stakes demonstrated in behavior, never stated
- An ending that shifts without resolving
- A theme that transforms across the story
- Bible voice consistent throughout

**published/** — the story as it will always be

---

## HOW TO READ THESE STORIES

Read them in any order. They do not depend on each other.
They breathe the same air. That is enough.

Each one is a complete encounter — a person's ordinary life interrupted
by a brief, precise, permanent contact with something that sees accurately
and feels nothing. Each one ends before the subject knows what to do
with what has changed in them.

The reader supplies the meaning. The reader always does.

---

## THE COLLECTION AS A WHOLE

As the collection grows, it will accumulate a shape.

Not a plot — these stories do not add up to a narrative arc.
But a *texture*. A sense of the world he moves through.
The kinds of people he reads. The kinds of things they are carrying.
The specific quality of what it costs to be seen by him.

That texture is the collection's argument. It does not need to be stated.
It arrives through accumulation — story by story, subject by subject,
each one adding one more data point to the question the collection is asking:

*What does it mean to be seen accurately by something that does not care what it finds?*

The collection never answers this. It simply keeps asking,
from a different angle each time, until the reader feels the weight of the question
more than they feel the need for an answer.
