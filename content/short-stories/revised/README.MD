# revised/
*Where raw drafts become stories. Where the AI earns its place.*

---

## WHAT THIS FOLDER IS

`revised/` receives files that are done being raw and not yet ready to be published.

The job here is surgical. Not creative from scratch — *clarifying* what already exists.
The author's impulse, their subject, their original idea: these are canon.
The revision process sharpens them into something a reader can hold.

A story enters `revised/` rough.
It leaves `revised/` shaped — voice consistent, structure present, quotes landed,
bible compliance confirmed — and moves to `published/`.

---

## THE REVISION BRIEF — READ THIS FIRST

Every file arriving in `revised/` must carry a revision brief at the top,
written by the author before handoff. It looks like this:

```
REVISION BRIEF
——————————————
What this story is trying to be:
[One or two sentences. The original impulse.]

What is working:
[Lines, images, ideas to preserve exactly.]

What is not working:
[What is unclear, missing, or wrong.]

What it needs:
[Specific revision tasks — see task list below]

Awareness level of subject:
[Level 0 / Level 1 / Level 2 / Level 3]

0013's presence tier:
[Direct / Peripheral / Absent / Voiced]

Format type:
[Fragment / Document / Scenario / Confession / Other]
```

If the brief is missing, **do not begin revision.**
Return the file to the author for a brief. Revision without a brief
produces a different story, not a better version of this one.

## THE REVISION TASKS

These are the four operations available in revision.
The brief specifies which apply. Apply only what is specified.
Do not add unrequested changes — they are not improvements, they are replacements.

---

### TASK 1 — FILL IN MISSING QUOTES AND SHARP LINES

Raw drafts often have placeholders where the hard language should be.
A section that gestures toward what 0013 says without committing to it.
A moment of being read that is described but not rendered.
An ending that trails off where it should land.

The job: write the lines that are missing.

0013's lines must follow the voice rules exactly:
- Specific observation, not vague insight
- No pause for effect — states and continues
- The weight lands after he has already moved on
- Clinical register — interested, not invested

Sharp lines for other characters must be equally specific:
- Not "she felt exposed" but the exact behavior of someone feeling exposed
- Not "he didn't know what to say" but the specific wrong thing he said anyway

**Test for a sharp line:** Could this line belong to a different story?
If yes, it is not sharp enough. Every line must be irremovable from this specific story.

---

### TASK 2 — FIX UNCLEAR OR INCOHERENT SECTIONS

Raw drafts written on a whim often have sections that made sense
in the moment of writing and do not hold together on re-reading.
Internal contradictions. Jumps in logic. A subject who behaves inconsistently.
A sequence of events that doesn't track.

The job: find what the author was reaching for and write toward it.

Rules:
- **Preserve the impulse, fix the execution.** If a section is unclear,
  ask what it was trying to do before deciding how to fix it.
  The revision brief's "what this story is trying to be" is the anchor.
- **Do not cut what is merely rough.** Rough is not wrong.
  Only cut what actively contradicts the story's logic or the bible.
- **Do not add new ideas.** Fix what exists. The author's ideas are the story.
  Your ideas are interference unless the brief specifically asks for them.

---

### TASK 3 — ENFORCE BIBLE VOICE AND RULES

The bible is the standard. Every revised story must pass it.
Read `bible/writing-rules.md` before applying this task.

Key checks:

**0013 is never romanticized.**
No penetrating gaze. No meaningful silences performed for effect.
No internal monologue unless the story is explicitly Voiced tier.
He is precise, interested, affectless. Never theatrical.

**The world is realist.**
No confirmed supernatural. No genre mechanics bleeding in.
The strangeness is always 0013 — not the world around him.

**The subject's interiority is specific.**
They do not feel *anxious.* They check the phone for the fourth time.
They cannot finish a sentence. They order something they don't want
because it gave them more time to decide something else.
Replace every generic emotional summary with a specific behavior or thought.

**The ending does not resolve.**
It shifts. The subject is different. We do not know if different is better.
We do not tell the reader what to feel about the difference.
If the draft ends on revelation or closure — cut back to the shift.

**The institution's voice, if present, is passive and redacted.**
Bureaucratic. Confident in tone, uncertain in content.
Strategic gaps where the most important information would be.

---

### TASK 4 — ADD ENCOUNTER STRUCTURE WHERE MISSING

Raw drafts sometimes have the subject and the moment but not the full arc.
A beautiful portrait of someone with no encounter. An encounter with no aftermath.
A moment of being read with no ordinary world preceding it.

The encounter structure is not a formula — it is a *shape.*
Apply only the sections that are missing. Do not rebuild what exists.

The shape, for reference:

```
1. Ordinary world      — the subject, specific and real, before anything happens
2. 0013 enters         — a quality before a person; no announcement
3. Moment of being read — quiet, specific, exact; he states and moves on
4. Exchange            — optional; only if the subject's response reveals something new
5. He leaves           — unremarkably; the absence is louder than the exit
6. Aftermath           — small, specific, unresolved; the shift, not the meaning of it
```

When adding missing sections, match the existing prose's register exactly.
The added sections should be indistinguishable from the author's voice.
If they are not, rewrite until they are.

## THE AWARENESS LEVELS — REVISION IMPLICATIONS

The subject's awareness level determines how the prose behaves.
Check the brief and apply the correct register throughout.

**Level 0 — Unaware**
The subject has no meta-awareness. The story is their life, interrupted.
They do not suspect they are being read by something larger than a strange man.
Prose is grounded, realist, entirely inside their ordinary interiority.

**Level 1 — Sensing**
The subject feels something is wrong about the encounter but cannot name it.
The prose carries a faint dissonance — things are slightly too precise,
the coincidences are slightly too apt. The subject notices but does not conclude.
They leave the story haunted by something they cannot file.

**Level 2 — Knowing**
The subject is a reader themselves. They recognize 0013 not by name
but by what he is — something that exists at the level of story, not person.
The prose can carry more weight. The subject knows they are inside something.
They do not know how to exit it. They may not want to.

**Level 3 — Addressing**
Used once in the entire collection, maximum.
The subject speaks outward — past 0013, past the story, toward the reader.
The prose breaks toward the fourth wall without breaking through it.
Handle with extreme care. This is the story that mirrors the novel's final chapter.

## THE PRESENCE TIERS — REVISION IMPLICATIONS

**Direct** — 0013 enters the scene. The encounter happens on the page.
Full encounter structure applies. His lines must be exact.

**Peripheral** — 0013 is at the edge. Mentioned, glimpsed, felt.
He may have a single line or none. His influence structures the scene
without him entering it. The subject is responding to his proximity, not his presence.

**Absent** — 0013 never appears. Pure aftermath.
The subject is living inside the permanent shift of a past encounter.
He is only present as a gap — something that happened before the story began.
This tier requires the most precise subject interiority.
There is nothing else carrying the weight.

**Voiced** — His narration bleeds into the prose without announcement.
The subject is telling their story. Somewhere in the middle, something changes.
The register shifts subtly — more precise, more cold, more interested.
The reader notices before the subject does.
The transition must be invisible on first read. On second read, unmissable.

## FORMAT TYPES — WHAT THEY ALLOW

The format changes per story. Each format has different rules for what is permitted.

**Fragment** — A moment. No full arc required. Can be one section of the encounter structure with everything else implied. Ends mid-motion if necessary.

**Document** — Institutional register. Report, case file, transcript, intake form.
Information delivered in passive voice with strategic redactions.
The horror is in what the document cannot see about itself.

**Scenario** — A contained event with stakes. Something happens beyond the encounter — a decision, a crisis, a situation — and 0013 is present inside it.
The most ORV-adjacent format. The encounter structure is present but the world is larger.

**Confession** — First person, present or past tense, the subject addressing someone.
Not 0013. Not the reader. Someone in their life they cannot say this to out loud.
0013's presence is felt through what they are confessing about.

## BEFORE MOVING TO published/

A story leaves `revised/` only when all of the following are true:

```
□ Revision brief tasks are complete
□ 0013 has no internal POV (unless Voiced tier — and even then, unmarked)
□ The moment of being read is specific — a behavior, not a feeling
□ The ending shifts, does not resolve
□ Bible voice is consistent throughout
□ Awareness level is legible in the prose — not stated, felt
□ Presence tier is consistent — no accidental tier-mixing
□ Subject logged in _index/character-appearances.md if named
□ Themes logged in _index/theme-tracker.md
□ Draft header updated: status: revised
□ Revision brief removed from the top of the file
```

When all boxes are checked: move to `published/`. Do not return to `raw/`.
Published is final. If something needs changing after publication,
log it in `_index/continuity-log.md` and address it in future entries.
