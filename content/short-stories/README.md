Use this template when writing a short story from inside a subject's perspective.

## [Title — can be left until final draft]

---

[OPEN IN THE MIDDLE]
The subject is already inside their life.
Do not introduce them. Do not set the scene from outside.
Begin inside a thought, a feeling, a moment already in motion.

The subject has an interior life. Use it from the first sentence.
What are they thinking right now — specifically?
What are they doing with their hands?
What is the small, specific texture of this ordinary moment?

Correct register:
> She had rearranged the salt shaker three times since sitting down
> and was reaching for it again before she caught herself.
> He was supposed to have texted by now. She had not checked her phone
> because checking would mean she was waiting, and she had decided
> twenty minutes ago that she was not waiting.

Wrong register:
> Sarah was a 34-year-old woman sitting in a café.
> She was feeling anxious about something.

---

[THE ORDINARY WORLD]
Before 0013 arrives, the subject's life must be real.
Give the reader two to four paragraphs inside this person.
Their specific situation. Their specific worry or hope or habit.
What they are carrying today that they are not saying out loud.

The more specific and real this section is,
the harder the interruption lands when it comes.
Do not rush this section to get to 0013.
The ordinary world earns everything that follows.

---

[0013 ENTERS]
He arrives. He is not announced. He is not introduced.
He is simply present — at the next table, the adjacent seat, the same counter.

The subject notices something about him.
Not his appearance fully — a quality. A stillness.
The specific sense of being in the presence of someone
who is paying a different kind of attention than people usually pay.

He does not introduce himself.
He does not perform friendliness.
He begins — with a question, an observation, a statement —
the way someone begins a sentence they have already finished in their head.

---

[THE MOMENT OF BEING READ]
This is the center of the story. Everything builds toward it.
Everything after it is consequence.

He says something exactly, uncomfortably true.
The subject has not said this aloud.
They have barely thought it clearly.
He has read it from data they did not know they were broadcasting.

This moment must be quiet.
The most devastating accuracy arrives without fanfare.
He does not pause for effect. He states and continues.
The weight lands after he has already moved on.

What this feels like from inside the subject:
Not violation — something closer to sudden transparency.
The specific discomfort of being seen accurately by a stranger.
The strange absence of the usual defenses,
because there is nothing to defend against.
He has not accused them of anything. He has only stated what is true.

Correct register:
> "You've checked your phone four times," he said, not looking up,
> "but you turn the screen away each time. Which means the messages
> arriving are from someone you don't want to think about
> while waiting for the one you do."
> She opened her mouth. She closed it.
> He was already looking out the window.

---

[THE EXCHANGE — optional]
The subject may respond. They may not.
If they respond, it does not change him.
He receives their response the way a system receives input —
it enters, it is processed, it does not alter the conclusion already reached.

The subject experiences this as uneven.
They are fully present. He is present in a different way —
attentive but not invested.
They are inside a conversation. He has already filed it.

---

[HE LEAVES]
He goes. Without drama. Without a designed exit line.
He stops and goes the way a person moves on from a completed task.

The subject watches or does not.
But they notice the absence.
The specific quality of the absence — not like a person leaving,
more like a particular kind of attention being withdrawn.
The room returns to its ordinary noise.
It is not the same room it was before he was in it.

---

[THE AFTERMATH]
What has shifted in the subject?
Small. Specific. Not resolved — shifted.

He has not fixed them. He has not broken them.
He has named something. In this world, named things have more narrative weight.
They are, in some way they cannot articulate, more real than they were before.

The story ends on the shift. Not on what they do with it.
Not on whether they are better or worse.
On the specific, small, undeniable fact of what is different now.

Correct register:
> She did not finish her coffee.
> She thought about what he had said for eleven days.
> She never told anyone, which was also, she understood eventually, a kind of answer.

Wrong register:
> She realized that she had been avoiding the truth all along.
> Now she felt ready to face her feelings and move forward with her life.
